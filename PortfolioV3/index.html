Background

Purdue University houses more than 50,000 students each year. With limited on-campus apartments and dormitories, students highly consider moving to off-campus properties.

Off-campus housing is just as hard to aquire and comes with a strenuous search process

In 2023 alone, students have camped overnight outside of property managment companies just for a shot at securing housing.

When searching for housing, sources of information lack consistency and comprehensive details, leaving students unsure about the reliability and suitability of their housing choices.

Students camping overnight for a chance at signing a lease. Read More

The Challenge

How Might We provide Purdue students, especially those who are new the search process, with accessible guidance and a centralized, dependable source of housing information generated from community discussion?

How Might We alleviate the challenges associated with making housing decisions based on limited, potentially biased, and inconsistent sources, such as word-of-mouth?

Understanding the Problem Space
Competitive Analysis

We looked into 4 different sites that allow users to find different housing options on and off campus to better understand the current tools that students use when searching for off-campus housing. We then compared each platform’s positives and negatives to see what opportunities we could find that could be incorporated into future potential solutions.

Codesign Workshop

Our team organized a codesign workshop with Purdue students that allowed us to:
1. Discover unique perspectives
2. Include user/stakeholder opinions and perferences in key design decisions
3. Design 'with' instead of just 'for' users/stakeholders
